> ## 2. Key Technical Concepts
> 
> - **Hybrid sigma-pressure coordinates**: `p_k = a_k + b_k · p_s`, generalising both pure sigma (a=0, b=σ) and pure pressure (a=p_k, b=0)
> - **DISCO divergence convention**: Only component 1 of velocity contributes to divergence; component 0 is the rotational/vortical part. Setting only `uv[..., 0]` gives zero divergence.
> - **sigma_dot bug**: Missing `∂ln(p_s)/∂t` term in σ̇ recurrence caused top-interface σ̇ = `∂ln(p_s)/∂t` instead of 0 (spurious mass flux through model lid)
> - **Sign convention in hybrid**: `delta_b = b_interface[k+1] - b_interface[k] < 0` (b decreases from 1 at surface to 0 at top), so `dp_k = delta_a + delta_b * p_s < 0`. The dp_s/dt formula uses the positive sign: `dp_s_dt = +∑(dp_k * div + delta_b * V·∇p_s)` (not negative)
> - **C interface recurrence**: `C_{k+1/2} = C_{k-1/2} - Δb_k ∂p_s/∂t - Δp_k div_k - Δb_k V_k·∇p_s` ensures `C_K = 0` at top
> - **Vertical derivatives in pressure space**: `∂X/∂p` via central finite differences using spatially varying `p_k = a_k + b_k p_s`
> - **PGF correction**: `-∇φ_k - (RT_k b_k / p_k) ∇p_s`
> - **Temperature tendency**: `∂T/∂t = -V·∇T + (κT/p)ω - C_k ∂T/∂p` where `ω = b_k(∂p_s/∂t + V·∇p_s) + C_k`

## What's already implemented

Three multi-level steppers are in `fme/core/shallow_water/`:

| Stepper | Coordinates | Prognostic state |
|---|---|---|
| `PrimitiveEquationsStepper` | Fixed isobaric (constant p) | `(uv, T, q)` |
| `SigmaCoordinateStepper` | Sigma (σ = p/p_s) | `(uv, T, q, p_s)` |
| `HybridCoordinateStepper` | Hybrid (p = a + b·p_s) | `(uv, T, q, p_s)` |

All three have: multi-level momentum advection (Lamb form: ∇KE − ζ×V), pressure gradient force from hydrostatic geopotential, Coriolis, vertical advection, and temperature as a full prognostic (not constant density). Humidity is a passive tracer. RK4 time integration.

## On constant density vs. temperature

You don't need constant density, and temperature is straightforward to include using the **hydrostatic approximation** — which is what all three steppers use. Here's why it works:

- The full compressible equations would require predicting density explicitly (ρ) alongside p and T.
- The hydrostatic approximation drops the vertical momentum equation (`∂w/∂t = 0`) and treats the vertical structure as being in hydrostatic balance: `∂φ/∂p = -RT/p` (or equivalently `∂φ/∂z = g`).
- This makes density *diagnostic* from the ideal gas law `ρ = p/(RT)` — you never need to carry it as a prognostic variable.
- Temperature enters through: (1) the geopotential integration (warm columns are taller → pressure gradient force), and (2) adiabatic compression heating `(κT/p)ω` in the thermodynamic equation.

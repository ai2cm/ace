## 4 ¬∑ Response curve + trajectory vs baseline `i26sidsm`

`band_gamma=1` sets band weights `‚àù (k/k_max)^1` (mean-1 normalized): **lo 0.33√ó /
mid ~1.0√ó / hi 1.7√ó** ‚Äî the strongest tail tilt of the sweep, but lo is still in the loss
(unlike `xgcaf2rt`'s hard cut). Comparison at each run's **best-sustained spectrum**
(rolling-median, window ~650 steps ‚Äî the fair, step- and noise-controlled read):

| `band_gamma` | run | bs step (frac) | lo | mid | hi | **mean** | tail99.99 / 99.9999 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 (flat) | `i26sidsm` | 2340 (8%) | **0.022** | 0.038 | 0.074 | 0.043 | 1.10 / 1.07 |

**Read (this arm vs flat `i26sidsm`):** the strong tilt gives the sweep's **largest hi-k
gain** ‚Äî hi 0.074‚Üí0.050 (‚àí32%) ‚Äî and the **best overall `spec_mean`** 0.043‚Üí0.035
(‚àí19%), with tails marginally closest to ideal. The cost is the **largest lo penalty**:
0.022‚Üí0.037 (+68%), making lo the worst band for this arm. `crps` tied. This is the
monotonic continuation of `gamma0p5`: more Œ≥ ‚Üí better hi, worse lo, slightly better mean.

**Convergence speed ‚Äî unchanged.** Spectrum reaches within 10% of best-sustained at step
2600, comparable to `i26sidsm` (2340) and `gamma0p5` (2340); `band_gamma` does not speed
or slow convergence.

**Checkpoint-selection trap (again).** `best_student_tail` landed at 10% (`spec_mean`
0.25), far from the spectral optimum at ~15% ‚Äî so the *deployed* checkpoint would be
spectrally poor. The mean gain is only visible at the best-sustained step, which no
current selector targets (motivates spec 13).

## Verdict

- **Outcome vs baseline `i26sidsm`:** ‚ûï **mild positive ‚Äî the strongest arm of the sweep
  on hi and mean.** At best-sustained spectrum: **hi 0.074‚Üí0.050 (‚àí32%), mean 0.043‚Üí0.035
  (‚àí19%)**, tails marginally best, `crps` tied. The trade is a **large lo cost** (+68%,
  now the worst band). It is the monotonic endpoint of the `{0, 0.5, 1}` response curve
  (¬ß4) ‚Äî the tilt helps the target band exactly as designed, and because lo is only
  down-weighted (0.33√ó) not zeroed, the net mean still improves (unlike the neutral
  hard-cut `xgcaf2rt`).
- **Recommended checkpoint:** best-sustained ~step 2600 (`spec_mean` 0.035). **Do not
  deploy `best_student_tail` @1690** ‚Äî it sits at an unconverged 0.25 spectrum. A
  spectral-aware selector (spec 13) is needed to capture the optimum.
- **Next action:** `gamma1` is the best-mean point of the sweep, but the gains over flat
  are small and **lo is now the limiting band** ‚Äî pushing Œ≥ higher would starve lo toward
  the neutral `xgcaf2rt` regime, so this is likely near the useful end of the monotonic
  ramp. The more promising lever is a **non-monotonic mid/hi bump** that lifts hi without
  down-weighting lo (needs a `SpectralMatchingLoss` change; `band_gamma` is monotonic).
  Confirm the Œ≥=1 gain at the spec-13-selected checkpoint + a held-out eval before
  adopting over flat.

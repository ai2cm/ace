# Run report — `ace-downscaling-distillation-fdistill-with-val-prate-spectral-gamma0p5`

_Hypothesis: the corrected spectral loss win `i26sidsm` weights every wavenumber
equally (`band_gamma=0`). The student's deficit is concentrated in the small-scale
tail, so a gentle high-k tilt should spend more of the (fixed-magnitude) spectral
budget there. `band_gamma=0.5` applies `band_weight_k ∝ (k/k_max)^0.5`, normalized to
mean 1, giving ≈ lo 0.61× / mid 1.06× / hi 1.37× — a **mild** tail emphasis that keeps
the low band meaningfully constrained (unlike the `min_wavenumber=85` cut `xgcaf2rt`,
which ~zeroed lo and came back neutral). Expectation: `spec_mae_hi_PRATEsfc` improves
vs `i26sidsm` without lo degrading the way the hard cut caused. This is the gentle
point of a `band_gamma ∈ {0, 0.5, 1}` ramp (0 = `i26sidsm`; ~2 ≈ `xgcaf2rt`)._
